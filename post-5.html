💕 Romanticizing My Life: A Beginner’s Guide

Because turning your Tuesday into a movie montage is totally valid — and yes, this is my Central Perk era.

✨ Step 1: Pretend You’re the Main Character

Think less “damsel in distress,” more Rachel Green in Season 6, walking into Ralph Lauren with a latte and self-worth. Whether you’re heading to Target or just reorganizing your skincare shelf, cue your mental soundtrack and give yourself a slow-mo moment.

“It’s not that common, it doesn’t happen to every girl, and it is a big deal.” — also me hyping myself up to check my to-do list.

📖 Step 2: Make Mundane Moments Magical

Monica would absolutely light a candle before scrubbing the baseboards, and you should too. Pour your matcha into a cute glass. Wear perfume even if no one’s around. Put your laundry in mesh bags like it’s high fashion. Joey doesn’t share food, but he sure as hell enjoys it — do the same.

Life’s too short not to romanticize your 3PM snack like it's a Michelin experience.

🌸 Step 3: Romanticize Rest Too

Channel Chandler’s sarcasm and take rest very seriously. A bubble bath with a face mask? That's not indulgent. That's preventative emotional maintenance. Lay back. Stare at the ceiling. Practice saying “Could I BE any more relaxed?” out loud.

🎀 Step 4: Create Tiny Traditions

Start a weekly solo coffee date. Light a candle every time you journal. Watch your comfort show (hint: Friends) while eating comfort food. Build little rituals that feel like home — even if your home is just your room and a playlist called “Soft Girl September.”

And if your “tradition” is crying to Phoebe’s “Smelly Cat” in your robe at 11PM… that’s sacred too.

🕯️ Step 5: Curate Your Energy

Hang out with people who make you laugh like Ross's awkward dinosaur jokes. Who would help you move a couch and yell “PIVOT!” with full commitment. Your vibe deserves to be protected like Monica protects her fancy wedding china.

Romanticizing your life means choosing joy, intention, and not settling for anything less than Joey-level loyalty.

💌 Closing Thoughts

I’m still learning how to be the main character. Some days I feel like background noise in someone else’s pilot episode. But more and more, I’m starting to feel like Season 3 Rachel — figuring it out, building a life, and learning to love myself.

I’m also working on something I’ve never been very good at: prioritizing me.
Not just in a bubble bath-and-face-mask kind of way (though those definitely help), but in the deeper sense — choosing myself, standing up for my needs, and not shrinking to fit into spaces that were never made for me.

And the best part? You’re coming with me.
If you’re reading this, you’re now part of my journey — the soft, sometimes messy, always-growing adventure of turning life into something beautiful, one day at a time.

So here's to romanticizing life — one walk, one playlist, one glass of wine (in a mug if needed) at a time.

And remember: I’ll be there for me. 💖
And maybe you will be too.
