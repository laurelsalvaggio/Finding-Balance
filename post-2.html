My First Coding Project - Building My Own Website from Scratch

Why a Website?

I wanted a space that was entirely mine—somewhere I could showcase projects, write about what I was learning, and experiment with new things as I grew. A personal website felt like the perfect starting point. It was practical, creative, and challenging enough to push me out of tutorial mode and into actual development.

Getting Started

I didn't have a clear plan in the beginning. I just knew I wanted it to look clean and feel like me. I started with the basics:

  • HTML for the structure
  • CSS for the design
  • A touch of JavaScript to make it interactive
I still remember writing my first <div> and styling it with some very questionable colors. But hey, it was mine—and it worked.

Challenges and Small Wins

There were plenty of frustrating moments. Layouts did't align, images didn't size properly, and don't get me started on trying to center a div. I Googled everything. I read Stack Overflow threads like they were survival guides. But every time I figured something out—no matter how small—I felt a genuine sense of accomplishment.

One big milestone was setting up responsive design. Seeing my website adjust smoothly on both a phone and desktop made me feel like I'd unlocked a superpower.

What I Built

The site started simple: a homepage, an about section, and a projects page. I added links, embedded images, and even experimented with subtle animations. As it came together, I saw not just a site—but my progress—on the screen.

It wasn't perfect, but it was functional, and more importantly, it reflected me.

What I Learned

Building my own website taught me more than just code. I learned how to:

  • Break down problems and think logically
  • Research and adapt when things didn't go as planned
  • Appreciate small progress over perfection

It was a reminder that learning to code isn't about knowing everything upfront—it’s about staying curious and persistent.

What's Next

This project was just the beginning. I'm already thinking about new features to add, maybe a blog section (like this one!), a contact form, or even integrating a CMS down the road.

If you've been thinking about starting your first coding project, let this be your sign: just start. You'll learn more than you expect—and you'll be proud you did.
